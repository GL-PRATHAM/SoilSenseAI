About this project

Where soil science meets artificial intelligence

Developed at the Department of Civil Engineering, Maharaja Institute of Technology Mysore, this project uses AI to make soil identification fast, accurate, and accessible to engineers, researchers, and farmers.

Three AI tools in one: upload a soil photo, enter your lab test values, or describe a planned structure — and get instant classification, quality assessment, and engineering advice.

"Understanding soil type is the foundational first step in any civil engineering project — from foundation design to road construction. This tool makes that step effortless."

— GL Pratham, Project Creator

Type 01
Red Laterite Soil
Rich in iron and aluminium oxides, giving a brick-red colour. Formed by intense tropical weathering over millions of years. The most dominant soil around Mysore and southern Karnataka.

🌾 Best Crops
  • Ragi (Finger Millet) — primary crop in Karnataka laterite zones
  • Cashew, Coffee, Tea — major plantation crops
  • Groundnut, Tapioca, Pineapple
  • Coconut in coastal areas
🏗️ Engineering Use
  • SBC: 150–250 kN/m² — moderate foundation suitability
  • Excellent road subgrade material when compacted
  • Used directly as road metal (WBM) in Karnataka PWD projects
  • Shallow strip/isolated footings generally adequate
⚠️ Challenges
  • Low fertility — needs NPK fertilizers
  • Hardens to brick-like consistency when dry
  • Susceptible to erosion on slopes
📍 Karnataka Locations
  • Mysore, Hassan, Kodagu, Dakshina Kannada, Udupi districts

Type 02
Black Cotton Soil (Regur)
Dark, highly expansive clay soil — swells dramatically when wet and develops deep shrinkage cracks when dry. One of the most challenging soils for civil engineering. Covers the Deccan Plateau extensively.

🌾 Best Crops
  • Cotton — thrives due to high moisture retention
  • Jowar (Sorghum), Wheat, Gram (Chickpea)
  • Sunflower, Sugarcane with irrigation
  • Oilseeds — linseed, safflower
🏗️ Engineering Use
  • SBC: 50–100 kN/m² — poor, highly variable
  • Mandatory: Under-reamed pile foundations (4–8 m depth)
  • Raft foundations needed for heavy structures
  • Road subgrade must be stabilized with lime or fly ash
  • Avoid shallow footings — extreme heave risk
⚠️ Challenges
  • Severe swelling (up to 300% volume change)
  • Foundation heave and settlement are major risks
  • Roads crack badly every monsoon season
  • Construction should avoid wet season where possible
📍 Karnataka Locations
  • Dharwad, Gadag, Vijayapura (Bijapur), Belagavi, Bagalkot — North Karnataka

  • Used as fine aggregate in concrete (river sand)
⚠️ Challenges
  • Low bearing capacity when loose — needs deep investigation
  • Scour risk near water bodies and bridges
  • Wind erosion in arid areas

Type 08
Peaty Soil
Dark, spongy soil formed from partially decomposed organic matter in waterlogged conditions. Highly acidic with extremely high water content. Found in Kodagu wetlands and coastal Kerala borders.

🌾 Best Crops (after treatment)
  • Blueberries, Cranberries — acid-loving plants
  • Azalea, Rhododendron (ornamental)
  • Rice in naturally waterlogged areas
🏗️ Engineering Use
  • SBC: <50 kN/m² — extremely poor, avoid if possible
  • Peat must be completely removed and replaced for any structure
  • Pile foundations to competent strata below peat layer
  • Surcharge preloading required if peat cannot be removed
⚠️ Major Risk
  • Extremely high consolidation settlement — can sink for years
  • Not suitable for any direct foundation without treatment

Type 11
Chalky / Limestone Soil
Pale grey to white, alkaline soil overlying chalk or limestone bedrock. Very free-draining, shallow, and stony. Causes yellowing (chlorosis) in acid-loving plants. Found in Vijayapura and Chitradurga limestone tracts.

🌾 Best Crops

  • Brassicas — cabbage, cauliflower, spinach
  • Wheat, Barley in deeper chalk soils
🏗️ Engineering Use
  • SBC: 200–500 kN/m² if on solid limestone bedrock — excellent
  • Check for karst features — caves and sinkholes below
  • Sulphate attack on concrete — use sulphate-resistant cement
  • Rock anchors and rock socketed piles work well
📍 Karnataka Locations
  • Vijayapura (Bijapur), Chitradurga, Bagalkot limestone outcrops

Type 12
Saline / Alkaline Soil
Soil with high salt content — white crust visible on surface. Found in arid and coastal regions, and areas with poor irrigation drainage. Hostile to most crops; causes severe corrosion of concrete and steel reinforcement.

🌾 Best Crops (salt-tolerant)

  • Coconut in coastal saline areas
  • Mangrove species in coastal wetlands
🏗️ Engineering Use
  • SBC varies widely — site investigation essential
  • Sulphate-resistant (SR) cement mandatory for all concrete
  • Epoxy-coated or stainless-steel reinforcement required
  • Cathodic protection for buried pipelines and piles
  • Concrete cover increased to 50–75 mm for durability
⚠️ Construction Risk
  • Severe corrosion of RCC structures — dramatically reduces lifespan
  • Chemical stabilization needed before use as fill
